Budgeting

Budgeting for teens is all about learning to control your money instead of letting it control you. At its core, budgeting means tracking how much money you have coming in (like pocket money, part-time job income, or gifts) and deciding where it should go before you spend it. An effective way to budget is by using the 50/30/20 rule: 50% of your money goes to needs (like transport, school supplies, or phone credit), 30% to wants (like clothes, games, or eating out), and 20% to savings (building up cash for big goals, emergencies, or investments). For teens, the key is to start small and be consistent—set a weekly or monthly spending plan, write down or use an app to track every dollar, and review it regularly. Over time, this habit builds discipline, helps you avoid debt, and sets you up with strong money skills before adult responsibilities kick in.
