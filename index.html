Budgeting

Budgeting for teens is all about learning to control your money instead of letting it control you. At its core, budgeting means tracking how much money you have coming in (like pocket money, part-time job income, or gifts) and deciding where it should go before you spend it. An effective way to budget is by using the 50/30/20 rule: 50% of your money goes to needs (like transport, school supplies, or phone credit), 30% to wants (like clothes, games, or eating out), and 20% to savings (building up cash for big goals, emergencies, or investments). For teens, the key is to start small and be consistent‚Äîset a weekly or monthly spending plan, write down or use an app to track every dollar, and review it regularly. Over time, this habit builds discipline, helps you avoid debt, and sets you up with strong money skills before adult responsibilities kick in.

Saving

Saving is setting aside a part of the money you earn or receive instead of spending it all right away. For teens, saving is powerful because it builds good money habits early, gives you a safety net for unexpected costs, and helps you reach goals like buying new gear, saving for a car, or even investing later. To save effectively, start small‚Äîput aside a set percentage of every dollar you get, like 20%. Keep savings separate from your spending money (like in a bank account or digital wallet) so you‚Äôre less tempted to use it. Always save for both short-term goals (like a new phone) and long-term goals (like education or starting a business). Saving is not just about storing money‚Äîit‚Äôs about building discipline and giving your future self more freedom and options.
